SEND today: a quick read for parents

Where things stand right now

The SEND system in England is under severe strain. Around 576,000 children have an Education, Health and Care Plan, a 140% increase since 2015.

The law is clear. The problem is delivery. Only around 60% of EHCPs are issued within the 20-week legal timescale. Many families wait six to twelve months.

Councils are in financial crisis. 95% are running SEND budget deficits.

What is actually changing

Some changes are no longer speculative. They have been publicly confirmed and funded.
- Government will write off 90% of council SEND deficits.
- £3.7 billion creating 60,000 new SEND places.
- Every secondary school expected to have an inclusion base.
- £200 million into SEND teacher training.
- National SEND standards being developed.

None of these confirmed changes remove existing legal rights.

What remains protected

The Children and Families Act 2014 is still the law. EHCPs are still legally enforceable. The 20-week timescale still applies. Parents still have the right to request assessment, choose a school, and appeal decisions.

What is actively being shaped

Alongside confirmed changes, there is active public debate.
- Four-tier model of support.
- Individual Support Plans (ISPs) from 2030 (proposed).
- Mandatory mediation before tribunal.
- Shifting funding to school clusters.

What is genuinely unknown

Key questions remain unanswered. No draft legislation has been published. No final national standards. No confirmed funding model replacing EHCP-linked funding.

What has been reported

There has been a steady flow of unconfirmed reports. None of these are government policy. None have legal force. A leak does not mean a decision has been made.

What happens next

- 23 February 2026: Schools White Paper published. Consultation open.
- Autumn 2026: Councils submit reform plans.
- By 2027: New Ofsted framework inspection.
- 2028-29: Central government takes funding responsibility.

What this means for you right now

Your child's rights today are real. They are enforceable. They do not disappear because of a headline.
- If your child needs an EHCP, the legal route has not changed.
- If in assessment, keep going.
- If provision is not delivered, challenge it.
