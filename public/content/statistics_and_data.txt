Statistics and data

Key SEND statistics for England as of 16 March 2026.

Summary facts
- SEND demand continues to rise (1.7m+ pupils).
- 638,745 active EHC plans as at January 2025.
- 97,747 new EHC plans started during 2024, a 15.8% increase on 2023.
- 154,489 requests for EHC needs assessment during 2024, an 11.8% increase.
- Fewer than half of new EHCPs completed within 20 weeks (46.4%).
- Tribunal activity at record levels: 25,000 appeals in 2024/25, over 95% won by parents.
- SEND spending increased, but funding gaps remain.

Confirmed position
- 638,745 Active EHC plans (Jan 2025)
- 10.8% Annual increase in active plans
- 97,747 New EHC plans started (2024)
- 15.8% Increase in new plans year-on-year
- 154,489 Assessment requests received (2024)
- 105,340 Assessments carried out (2024)
- 46.4% Issued within 20 weeks (2024), down from 50.3% (2023)
- 25,000 Appeals registered (2024/25)
- Over 95% Tribunal cases won by parents
- 639,000 Total young people (0-25) with EHCPs

Funding
- High needs funding: £9.4bn (2024/25) vs £5.3bn (2014/15)
- Local authorities spent £950m additional on top of allocation.
- Councils hold an estimated £6bn in high needs deficits.

Projected figures
- Annual DSG deficit projected to exceed £6bn in 2028/29.
- EHCP rate projected to rise to 7.7% by 2029, then fall to 4.7% by 2034-35.
- Around 1 in 8 children currently on EHCPs projected to transition to ISPs between 2030 and 2035.
