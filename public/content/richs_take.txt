Rich's Take

This isn't a neutral page. It is my personal view as a SEND dad and neuroinclusion consultant.

We welcome this. And we mean it.
Two documents. Billions of pounds. A government saying plainly that the SEND system has been failing. That acknowledgement is not nothing.

Is SEND the problem? Or is it the system that keeps creating it?
SEND costs are rising. EHCPs doubled. Why? If the answer is that the system is no longer fit for the children it serves, money won't fix it.

We built a system in the 1890s. We are still running it.
The education system was designed for a different world. The rise in SEND is a crisis of fit.

Then we gave children dopamine on demand.
Smartphones and sleep deprivation are part of the conversation.

The architecture of the new system has a real hole in it.
We welcome ISPs in principle. But they are school-led with no Tribunal route. The Tribunal has been the single most effective protection families have had.

Neurodivergent children are almost invisible in this document.
The word neurodivergent does not appear in the white paper. ADHD is mentioned twice. Masking is not mentioned.

And then we blamed the parents.
The system did not fail because parents asked for too much. It failed because it gave too little.

My honest verdict.
The ambition is right. The scale of investment is real. But we are trying to fix a SEND crisis without asking why it exists.
