Free Help in Your Area

It is called SENDIASS. Sometimes IASS. Sometimes by a local name entirely. Most parents have never heard of it.

What SENDIASS does

SENDIASS provides free, impartial, legally-based advice and support to:
- children and young people with SEND
- their parents and carers

It covers education, health, and social care, across the 0 to 25 age range. It is funded by the local authority but is legally required to operate independently of it.

What you can use it for

- Understanding the EHCP process
- Help preparing for meetings
- Understanding your rights
- Support preparing for mediation
- Help writing letters
- Signposting to tribunal support

What it costs

Nothing. It is free at the point of use.

How to find yours

Every LA area has one. The national finder is run by the Council for Disabled Children.
Search for "Find your local IASS" online.

If your SENDIASS is hard to reach

SENDIASS services are unevenly resourced. If you are struggling to get help, national organisations like IPSEA, SOS!SEN, and Contact can also help.

A note on independence

SENDIASS is funded by the local authority but must by law operate impartially. That means it should give you honest advice even when that advice involves challenging your local authority's decisions.
