What We Owe Our Children

The system is broken. But this page is not about the system. It is about us. What we can do.

The people trying to help your child are not your enemies

Teachers and SENCos are often working in conditions that make doing the job well very hard. Walking in assuming everyone is your adversary makes things harder. Build relationships where you can.

Prepare before you ask

Know what you believe your child needs. Not just that something is wrong — but what specifically is happening, when, and what you think is driving it.

Build an evidence jacket

A running record of your child's experience. Date. Situation. What happened. Over time a pattern becomes visible. A pattern is evidence.

Understand what is happening at home

Your child is almost certainly masking at school. The explosion at the school gate is the accumulated cost of hours spent masking. It is not bad behaviour.

Stimming

Stimming is regulation. If your child is being stopped from stimming, the regulatory need accumulates and finds another outlet.

Screens, sleep, and dopamine

Neurodivergent children are vulnerable to dopamine reward loops in gaming and social media. Sleep is not optional. A child who sleeps poorly will struggle to learn and regulate.

Understanding your child's profile

- Rejection Sensitive Dysphoria (RSD): Intense pain triggered by perceived rejection.
- Pathological Demand Avoidance (PDA): Everyday demands trigger extreme anxiety.
- Sensory Processing Differences (SPD): The world works at a different volume.
- Oppositional Defiant Disorder (ODD): Often a description of distress, not defiance.
