What to do right now

This page is for parents whose child is not getting the support they need today. Not next year. Now.

First: know who is responsible

The local authority (LA) is legally responsible for your child's EHCP. Not the school. Not the SENCo. The LA.

If provision is not being delivered, the complaint goes to the LA.

What is going wrong? (Common situations)

Situation 1: Provision in the EHCP is not being delivered
Your child has an EHCP. Section F is a legal duty.
1. Speak to the SENCo and headteacher. Keep a written record.
2. If nothing changes in 1-2 weeks, write formally to the LA (use IPSEA model letter).
3. If unresolved, escalate to the LGSCO.
4. If the EHCP is too vague, request an early annual review.

Situation 2: The LA has refused to assess your child for an EHCP
You have the right to appeal to the SEND Tribunal.
You must contact a mediation adviser for a certificate first (mediation itself is optional). You have two months to appeal.

Situation 3: The LA is taking too long
The legal timescale is 20 weeks. If missed, write formally to the LA. Escalate to LGSCO if needed.

Situation 4: You disagree with what is in the EHCP
You can appeal to the SEND Tribunal about sections B, F, or I. Mediation certificate required first.

Situation 5: Annual review has not happened
The LA must review at least every 12 months. Write to the LA asking them to schedule one. You can request an early review if needs change.

Keep a paper trail

Whatever situation you are in, keep written records.
- Date every phone call and note what was said
- Follow up verbal conversations with a brief email
- Keep copies of all letters/emails
- Note dates when deadlines were missed

Where to get help right now

- IPSEA: Free legally based advice, model letters, tribunal support.
- SOS!SEN: Free telephone helpline, tribunal support.
- Your local SENDIASS: Free, impartial advice funded by your LA.

A note on reform

Everything on this page is based on the law as it stands today. The Children and Families Act 2014 has not been amended. EHCPs remain legally enforceable. Your right to appeal to the SEND Tribunal has not changed.
