Why Where You Live Matters: Local Authority Variation in SEND

The support a child with SEND receives in England depends substantially on where they live. Two children with identical needs, living in different local authority areas, may receive entirely different levels of support.

What the inspection evidence says

Between January 2023 and December 2024, Ofsted and the CQC completed 54 full area SEND inspections.
- 14 areas: Positive experiences and outcomes
- 26 areas: Inconsistent experiences and outcomes
- 14 areas: Widespread or systemic failings

Fewer than one in four areas inspected received the top outcome. The system as a whole is not failing uniformly. The pattern is uneven.

What drives the variation

Financial pressure:
Local authorities fund SEND through the high needs block. Demand has doubled. Funding has not kept pace. Accumulated deficits reached £3.3 billion by 2024/25.

Safety Valve agreements:
The DfE has Safety Valve agreements with around 40 LAs carrying high deficits. These LAs receive funding in exchange for reforms to reduce spending. Critics argue this puts downward pressure on EHC plan issuing and provision.

Workforce and geography:
Access to educational psychologists and therapists varies by region. Rural areas face longer waits.

What it means for parents

- You are not imagining the inconsistency.
- Your LA's inspection outcome is public (Ofsted).
- Knowing your LA's Safety Valve status provides context for their decisions.

Legal rights

Local authority financial pressure does not reduce your legal rights.
A local authority cannot lawfully refuse to issue an EHC plan or reduce provision on the grounds of cost or budget pressure alone. (R v East Sussex County Council ex parte Tandy [1998]).

Considering moving

Moving local authority area is a significant decision.
If you have an EHCP and move:
- The new LA must adopt the plan.
- They must provide the provision in it (or equivalent) immediately.
- They have 6 weeks to decide whether to review or reassess.
- They cannot simply tear it up.

If you are in the assessment process:
- The old LA should transfer the information.
- The new LA should pick up where the old one left off.
