You Are Carrying a Lot

This page is not about your child. It is about you.

Parenting a neurodivergent child through a system that was not built for them is an exhausting and sometimes isolating experience. Research shows parents of children with SEND report significantly higher rates of anxiety, depression, and burnout.

What this experience actually costs

The forms. The waiting. The meetings where you feel dismissed. The hypervigilance. The grief for the version of parenthood you expected. And underneath all of it, the love.

This is not bad parenting. It is the cost of caring deeply inside a system that does not care enough.

You cannot sustain this alone

The parents who last longest are the ones who found other people who understood. Parent carer forums, local groups, online communities. This is survival infrastructure.

Parent Carer Forums

Every local authority area in England has a Parent Carer Forum. They are independent groups with a formal role. They are also communities of people who understand.

National organisations that support parents

- Contact: Charity for families with disabled children. Helpline, guides.
- Carers UK: Carer rights, financial guidance, mental health.
- Scope: Online community and helpline.

Your rights as a carer

Under the Care Act 2014, if you provide care for a disabled person, you have the right to a carer's assessment from your local authority. This is separate from your child's assessment. It can result in practical support or respite.

When you need more than a forum

If anxiety does not lift, sleep does not recover, or you feel a persistent sense of dread, your GP is the first route. The impact of caring is a legitimate presenting concern.

For the moments that feel darkest

Samaritans: 116 123 (Free, 24 hours). This is not just for crisis. It is for anyone who needs to talk.

A note from the person who built this site

You are not failing. The system is failing. Understanding that distinction changes the question from "what am I doing wrong" to "what does this system owe my child, and how do I get it".
