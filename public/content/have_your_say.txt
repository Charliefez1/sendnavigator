How To Have Your Say on SEND Reform

On 23 February 2026, the government published the Schools White Paper, Every Child Achieving and Thriving. This sets out the biggest change to SEND in a generation.

The government has confirmed that a formal 12-week public consultation will follow. That consultation window has not opened yet, but it is expected in 2026.

This page explains what a consultation response actually is, and how to write one that carries weight.

What has already happened

The DfE ran a national conversation on SEND reform between December 2025 and January 2026. That window has closed. The White Paper is now published.

What is coming next

Now that the White Paper has been published, the government will open a formal consultation on its proposals. During that window, you can submit a written response. Responses are read. They are counted.

Likely topics:
- Individual Support Plans (ISPs) from 2030
- Changes to EHCP eligibility
- Experts at Hand programme
- National SEND standards
- SEND Tribunal future
- Funding shift to central government

Why responding matters

Government is legally required to consider responses before finalising policy. The 2022 Green Paper consultation received over 22,000 responses, which shaped the subsequent plan.

How to write a response that carries weight

1. Be specific about your experience. "My child waited 27 weeks" is better than "it's too slow".
2. Respond to the actual questions.
3. Use evidence where you have it.
4. Keep it readable.
5. Say what you want, not just what is wrong.
6. You do not need to answer every question.

Other ways to influence reform

- Parliamentary petitions (e.g. Save Our Children's Rights)
- Contact your MP
- Giving evidence to parliamentary committees
